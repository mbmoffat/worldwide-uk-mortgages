**Project:** Worldwide UK Mortgages (working name)

**Version:** 0.1

## Methodology

This is a structural keyword map built from SERP analysis of the top-ranking pages for UK expat and seafarer mortgage queries, plus mortgage broker domain knowledge of borrower intent. Volumes are not attached. Volumes will be validated in the Ahrefs web UI for the Phase 2 build set before any page is committed.

## 1. Competitive landscape

### Lender-owned ranking pages
HSBC Expat, Skipton International, Suffolk Building Society, Family Building Society, NatWest International. Smaller niche lenders also visible: Marsden BS, Saffron BS, Tipton & Coseley, Newcastle BS.

### Specialist broker ranking pages
Strive Mortgages, Mortgage Lane, Mortgage London, Willow Private Finance, Clifton Private Finance, Kite Mortgages, Cleerly, Kerr & Watson, RockHopper, Freelancer Financials, Fox Davidson, Mint Mortgage & Protection, Expat Mortgages UK, SeeMoney.

### Content / aggregator sites
Experts For Expats, Online Mortgage Advisor, Mortgage Adviser Directory.

### Strategic implication
The lender pages can't cover anything beyond their own products. The big content sites own the head terms. Brokers cluster around procedural and category-led pages. Country-led, currency-led, and seafarer-profession long-tail pages are thinly covered or absent. That is the defensible surface area for this site.

## 2. Page architecture

Pages are tiered by build priority:
- **P1** Build in Phase 2. Hub or high-conversion landing page.
- **P2** Build in Phase 3 once P1 pages are indexed. Long-tail with strong intent.
- **P3** Validate volume in Ahrefs first. Speculative or thin demand.

### Tier 3a – Country-led expat pages (P2)

Pattern: `/expat-mortgages/[country]`. Each page covers lender appetite for borrowers based in that country, the dominant employer types and currencies, residency considerations, and typical deposit norms.

**Priority countries** (UK expat population plus lender appetite):
UAE / Dubai, Singapore, Hong Kong, USA, Australia, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Switzerland, Germany, Netherlands, Norway, Canada, New Zealand, South Africa.

**Lower priority** (validate in Ahrefs first):
Bahrain, Oman, Kuwait, France, Spain, Japan, Thailand. France and Spain skew retiree, lower mortgage demand. Japan and Thailand have lender restrictions that reduce conversion probability.

### Tier 3b – Currency-led pages (P2)

Pattern: `/foreign-currency-mortgage/[currency]`. Each page covers which UK lenders accept that currency, typical haircut applied, evidence requirements, comparison to GBP affordability.

Priority order:
USD, EUR, AED, SGD, HKD, CHF, SAR, QAR, AUD, NOK, CAD.

NOK has a strong tie-in with offshore oil and gas seafarer pages.

## 5. Long-tail strategy

The long-tail in this niche splits into two patterns:

1. **Procedural questions.** Example: "how to get an expat mortgage from Dubai". Article content works. Intent is information seeking. Ranking is incremental.

2. **Lender and criteria questions.** Example: "which UK lenders accept AED income". High intent, often pre-conversion. A programmatic page approach works here. Each currency or country gets a page following the same template, populated with actual lender behaviour for that profile. This is the kind of content the broader UK broker market has not built. Defensible. Slow to copy.

Pattern 2 is the Phase 3 content scale opportunity.
