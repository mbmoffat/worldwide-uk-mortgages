## Information we collect

We collect personal information in two ways: information you give us directly, and information collected automatically when you use the site.

### Information you provide directly

When you submit an enquiry through our contact form, we collect:

- Your first name
- Your contact telephone number or WhatsApp number
- Your email address
- Your country of residence
- Any additional information you choose to share in the message field

You provide this information voluntarily when you choose to submit an enquiry.

### Information collected automatically

When you visit the site, we automatically collect limited technical information through standard server logs and analytics tools. This may include:

- Your IP address
- The type of device, browser, and operating system you use
- The pages you view and the time you spend on each
- The site or source that referred you to our site
- The general geographic location associated with your IP address (country and region, not precise location)

This information is collected through cookies and similar technologies. See our Cookies Policy for details.

## Why we collect your information

We use the information you provide to:

- Respond to your enquiry and pass it to a suitable FCA-authorised mortgage broker for the regulated conversation
- Communicate with you about your enquiry, including any follow-up questions
- Maintain a record of enquiries received for our own business purposes

We use the technical information we collect automatically to:

- Operate, secure, and maintain the site
- Understand how visitors use the site so we can improve it
- Detect and prevent fraud or misuse

## Lawful basis for processing your information

Under UK data protection law, we must have a lawful basis to process your personal information. Our lawful bases are:

- **Consent**, where you have given us permission to use your information for a specific purpose. You can withdraw consent at any time.
- **Legitimate interests**, where we have a genuine interest in processing your data and our interests do not override your rights. This applies to our use of technical site analytics.
- **Legal obligation**, where we are required to retain or process information to comply with the law.

When you submit an enquiry, your consent is the lawful basis for sharing your details with the broker who will contact you.

## Who we share your information with

We share the personal information you provide in your enquiry with the FCA-authorised mortgage broker we introduce you to. The broker will use your information to contact you about your mortgage enquiry, and any further use is governed by the broker's own privacy policy, which they will share with you when they make contact.

We may also share your information with:

- Service providers who help us operate the site (for example, hosting providers, form submission services). These providers act under contract and process your information only on our instructions.
- Legal or regulatory authorities, where we are required to do so by law.

We do not sell your personal information to anyone.

We do not share your information with marketing companies or advertising networks.

## International transfers

Some of the service providers we use (for example, hosting infrastructure) may store or process your information outside the UK. Where this happens, we ensure the transfer is protected by appropriate safeguards as required by UK data protection law, such as standard contractual clauses or transfers to countries deemed adequate by the UK government.

## How long we keep your information

We keep your enquiry information for as long as is necessary to:

- Respond to your enquiry and complete the introduction
- Comply with any legal, regulatory, or accounting requirements
- Resolve disputes or enforce our rights

In practice, this means we typically retain enquiry data for up to six years from the date of the enquiry. After this period we will delete or anonymise the information.

Technical site data collected through analytics is typically retained for shorter periods, as set by the analytics provider.

## Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the following rights in relation to your personal information:

- **The right to be informed** about how we use your data (which this policy covers).
- **The right of access** to a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
- **The right to rectification** of inaccurate or incomplete information.
- **The right to erasure** of your information in certain circumstances ("the right to be forgotten").
- **The right to restrict processing** of your information in certain circumstances.
- **The right to data portability**, allowing you to obtain and reuse your information.
- **The right to object** to certain types of processing, including direct marketing.
- **The right to withdraw consent** at any time, where we rely on consent as our lawful basis.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at enquiry@ukmortgagesworldwide.com.

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you are unhappy with how we handle your personal information. The ICO can be contacted at ico.org.uk or by telephone on 0303 123 1113.

## Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include encrypted connections to the site (HTTPS), restricted access to enquiry data, and the use of reputable service providers.

No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure. While we use reasonable measures to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## Children

This site is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 18. If you believe a child has submitted information to us, please contact us and we will delete it.

We may update this policy from time to time. The "last updated" date at the top of the policy will reflect any changes. Material changes will be highlighted on the site for a reasonable period after they take effect.
