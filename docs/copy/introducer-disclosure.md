## What this page is

This page explains the role of UK Mortgages Worldwide and its relationship with the FCA-authorised mortgage brokers who provide regulated advice to people who submit enquiries through this site.

We want this to be clear so you understand exactly what we do, what we do not do, and what to expect when you submit an enquiry.

## Our role

UK Mortgages Worldwide is operated by [ENTITY NAME TBD], a company registered in England and Wales.

We are an information and lead-generation website. We are not regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and we do not give regulated advice on mortgages or any other financial products.

What we do:

- Publish general information about UK mortgages for British nationals living abroad, non-UK residents, foreign nationals, and applicants with foreign-currency income.
- Capture enquiries from people who want to discuss UK mortgages with a specialist broker.
- Pass those enquiries to FCA-authorised mortgage brokers who can provide regulated advice and arrange mortgages.

What we do not do:

- Provide regulated mortgage advice or recommendations.
- Compare specific mortgage products on your behalf.
- Make decisions about which lender or product is right for you.
- Submit mortgage applications.
- Hold or handle any client money.
- Charge you any fee for using this site or for the introduction.

## The broker who will contact you

When you submit an enquiry through our contact form, your details are passed to an FCA-authorised mortgage broker. That broker will contact you to discuss your situation.

The broker is regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority and is responsible for:

- Conducting the regulated advice conversation
- Recommending suitable mortgage products if appropriate
- Disclosing any fees they charge
- Providing the documentation required by FCA rules (Initial Disclosure Document, illustration, suitability letter, and so on)
- Submitting any application to the lender

You can verify the broker's authorisation on the FCA register at register.fca.org.uk before agreeing to proceed with regulated advice.

## Commercial relationship

We have a commercial relationship with the broker(s) we introduce you to. This means:

- We may receive a fee or share of commission from the broker for introducing you, regardless of whether you proceed with a mortgage application.
- The broker's fee disclosure document will set out any fees they charge you.
- You do not pay anything to us. Our fee comes from the broker, not from you.

We disclose this commercial relationship up front so you can make an informed decision. You are under no obligation to use the broker we introduce you to. If you would prefer to find a broker independently, you are free to do so.

## How introductions work

When you submit an enquiry:

1. Your details (name, contact information, country, and the message you provide) are submitted to our system.
2. We pass your enquiry to an FCA-authorised broker who specialises in expat and non-resident UK mortgages.
3. The broker contacts you, usually within a few hours, to discuss your situation.
4. The broker takes you through their own client onboarding, including any disclosure documents and fee agreements.
5. From that point, your relationship is with the broker. We are not part of the regulated advice conversation.

If the broker is not the right fit for you, or if your case is outside their typical scope, they may pass it back to us so we can introduce you to a different broker. You can also withdraw at any point and seek a different broker independently.

## Your data

When we pass your enquiry to a broker, we share your name, contact details, country of residence, and the contents of the message you submitted. We do not share any technical or analytics data about your visit to the site.

The broker becomes a separate data controller in relation to your information at that point. Their use of your information is governed by their own privacy policy, which they will share with you when they make contact.

For full details about how we handle your information, see our [Privacy Policy](/privacy).

## What we do not provide

To be unambiguous about the limits of our role:

- We are not authorised by the FCA. You will not find this entity on the FCA register, because the activities we undertake (publishing information and making introductions) do not require authorisation.
- We do not assess your individual circumstances, affordability, or suitability for any mortgage product.
- We do not promise or imply that any specific lender will lend to you, that any specific rate will be available, or that any application will succeed.
- The information on the site is general. It is not personalised to your situation. Your situation needs to be assessed by an FCA-authorised broker before any decisions are made.

## Complaints

If you are unhappy with anything about your interaction with UK Mortgages Worldwide, please contact us at enquiry@ukmortgagesworldwide.com. We will respond as quickly as we can and aim to resolve concerns directly.

If your concern relates to the regulated advice you received from the broker we introduced you to, the broker has its own complaints procedure as required by FCA rules. They will provide it on request. You also have the right to escalate complaints about regulated firms to the Financial Ombudsman Service at financial-ombudsman.org.uk.
